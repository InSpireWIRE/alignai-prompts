Frequently Asked Questions

What is an AI prompt?

An AI prompt is a written instruction you give to an AI model like Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini or Copilot to produce a specific result. A good prompt spells out the role, task, context, constraints and format — so the AI returns the answer you actually need the first time, not a generic one.

How do small businesses use AI prompts?

Small businesses use AI prompts to automate repeatable written work: marketing emails, product descriptions, job postings, customer replies, SOPs, cash-flow summaries and outreach messages. You copy the prompt, paste it into your AI tool, replace the bracketed placeholders with your own details, and run it.

Which AI model is best for small business?

There is no single best model. Claude tends to excel at long-form writing and reasoning, ChatGPT is strongest for general purpose work and custom GPTs, Gemini is well suited for Google Workspace users, and Copilot is built for Microsoft 365. Every prompt in this hub is tested across all four so you can pick the model you already pay for.

Are the prompts free?

Yes. Every prompt is free to copy and use. You unlock the full library by entering your email. No password, no paid plan, no credit card required.

How often are the prompts updated?

Every prompt is re-verified monthly and rebuilt whenever a major AI model changes. The homepage and each prompt page shows the last verified month, so you always know whether a prompt was tested against the current model.

Do I need a paid AI account?

No. The prompts work on the free tier of Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini and Copilot. Paid tiers give you longer context windows and faster responses, but the prompts are written to work on free plans.

What makes AlignAI prompts different from free prompt lists on the internet?

Most public prompt lists were written for older AI models and are never updated. AlignAI field tests each prompt, scores output quality, versions the prompt per model (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot), and republishes when a model changes. You get a prompt verified for the current version of the tool you are using, not a 2023 template.
