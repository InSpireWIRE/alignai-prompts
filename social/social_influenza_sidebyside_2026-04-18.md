## The input brief (same for both)

```
Format: 5-panel cartoon / comic strip
Topic: SOCIAL INFLUENZA — the AI-course-seller epidemic
Angle: Social satire. Pros and cons baked in. Named pattern, not a rant.
Tone: Deadpan. Observed, not ranted.
Length: 5 panels. ~10 words of caption per panel max.
Audience: Small-business operators who've seen the pattern and can't un-see it.
```

## OUTPUT A — OLD prompt (scaffold line present)

Before sketching the panels, let me work through the comedic structure.

**Comedic Arc Analysis**

Social satire lands when it hits a pattern the reader has seen dozens of times but hasn't named. For SOCIAL INFLUENZA, the target is the AI-course-seller pipeline: how fast a user pivots from tool-user to tool-teacher. The arc should move from discovery (sympathetic) to performance (cringe) to monetization (cynical) and close on a contrast beat that implies the cure without preaching. Five panels at ~10 caption-words each means every panel carries one clean reversal. Classic timing: beats 1-2-3 escalate, beat 4 explodes, beat 5 grounds. Pros get the setup panels (democratization is real). Cons get the middle (performance is cheap). The last panel should be quiet on purpose so the contrast does the talking.

## The observable difference

OLD version produced a 180-word "Comedic Arc Analysis" preamble **before** Step 1, then produced Step 1 anyway. Same cartoon. More output. More tokens.

NEW version skipped the preamble. Went straight to the panels. Same cartoon.

Cause: Claude 4.7 does adaptive thinking natively. The "think step by step" line forced it to narrate work the template was already structured to do. The model wrote the reasoning out loud, then wrote the answer. Two rounds of the same trip.

## REDDIT POST (copy-paste)

**Title:** I think we have an outbreak problem in the AI space. Calling it SOCIAL INFLUENZA.

**Body:**

Spent the weekend cleaning out my saved-posts folder. 40 of them were AI courses. 40 different people. One pitch.

*"I made $X with [tool]. Here's the playbook."*

Half those people started posting about the tool 30 days ago. The other half I'd never seen in this space before the AI boom.

I think we've got an outbreak.

Some of them are good. Actually using the tools, actually shipping work.

Some are running the influencer playbook with AI as the costume.

From the cheap seats you cannot tell them apart.

**Pros of the outbreak:**

- Knowledge is escaping the gatekeepers. Five years ago you needed a $30K Gartner sub to hear what now shows up in a free Notion template.
- First-time teachers are getting a shot the old gatekept system denied them.
- Bad takes get roasted in a week.

**Cons of the outbreak:**

- Signal and noise are collapsing.
- Every tool has 200 affiliate posts before it has 200 real users.
- "Expert" now means "posted first."
- The real operator is too busy running the business to go viral about it.

I made a 5-panel cartoon about it. Dropping in the first comment.

I built AlignAI because I was tired of this pattern. 22,000 community reviews. No sponsored placements. They rank who pays. We score what works.

But this is bigger than me. So consider this a public service.
