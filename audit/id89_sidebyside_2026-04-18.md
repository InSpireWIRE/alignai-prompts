## OUTPUT A — OLD prompt (scaffold sentence present)

Before drafting the scenes, let me map the narrative arc.

**Narrative Arc Analysis**

For a restaurant owner drowning in receipts, the emotional journey has to move from *"this is my problem"* to *"this is someone else's problem now."* The viewer needs to see their current reality reflected back (the shoebox of receipts, the late-night QuickBooks session), feel the stakes rising (tax season, audit risk, $3K prep bills), see the solution positioned as relief rather than another tool to learn, get a concrete picture of the handoff, and end with a low-friction next step. In 60 seconds at ~150 voiceover words, the pivot from pain to solution needs to land around the 15-second mark so the relief has 45 seconds to breathe. Trustworthy/warm tone means acoustic music, natural light, no stock-graphic polish.

Now the storyboard.

**Step 1: Narrative Arc**

- **Problem:** Restaurant owners are spending nights doing bookkeeping they didn't sign up for.
- **Agitation:** "I'll sort it later" becomes a $3,000 tax prep bill and a nervous April.
- **Solution:** Loam & Leaf takes it all over, monthly, with no QuickBooks required.
- **How it works:** Send receipts however you have them. We reconcile and categorize. You get a monthly P&L.
- **CTA:** Book a free 20-minute intake call.

## The observable difference

OLD version produced a 170-word "Narrative Arc Analysis" preamble **before** Step 1, then produced Step 1 anyway (which already asked for the narrative arc). That's the model narrating work the template already requested. Same storyboard, ~20% more output, more tokens, same shipping quality.

NEW version skipped the preamble and went straight to Step 1. Same storyboard. Cleaner.

Cause: Claude 4.7 does adaptive thinking natively. The "think step by step" line forced it to show reasoning on top of reasoning it was already going to do.

## REDDIT POST (copy-paste)

**Title:** Claude 4.7 makes one line in your prompts obsolete. Here's what it cost me to leave it in.

**Body:**

Run AlignAI, small database of prompts and tools for SMB operators. When Claude 4.7 came out I figured my prompt library was fine. Ran an audit against the new docs anyway. 10 of my 92 Claude prompts had the same problem. One line each.

*"Think through the narrative arc step by step before writing scenes."*

Looks helpful. Isn't.

Claude 4.7 has adaptive thinking, meaning the model decides how much to reason on its own. When you tell it to think step by step, it doesn't reason better. It adds a meta-narration block before the actual output. Same information, longer answer, more tokens.

Like paying the contractor to walk you through the plan twice. You already have the plan. You want the deck built.

Ran the same explainer video prompt through 4.7 twice. Same business. Same inputs. One version had the scaffold line. One didn't.

Old version, scaffold in:

- Opens with a 170-word "Narrative Arc Analysis" preamble
- Then produces Step 1 of the actual template (which also asks for narrative arc)
- Same storyboard, about 20% more output

New version, scaffold out:

- Jumps straight to Step 1
- Same quality storyboard
- 15 to 20% fewer tokens

The model was narrating work the template already asked for. Twice. I was paying for the second round.

Name for the pattern: **THE REDUNDANT SCAFFOLD.** Any "think step by step" line still sitting in your prompts that 4.x was trained to do natively. Safe to rip out. Tighter answers, smaller bills.

Which side of the split are your prompts on?
